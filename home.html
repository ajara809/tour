Let's see our Places


Goa


Goa, a small state on the western coast of India, is renowned for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage. It seamlessly blends Indian and Portuguese influences, evident in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Goa offers a diverse range of experiences, from relaxing on sun-kissed shores and exploring lush hinterlands to enjoying lively music festivals and savoring delectable seafood. Its laid-back atmosphere and friendly locals make it a popular destination for tourists seeking both relaxation and adventure.

Kodaikanal


Kodaikanal, often referred to as the "Princess of Hill Stations," is a charming hill town in the southern Indian state of Tamil Nadu. Nestled in the Palani Hills of the Western Ghats, it is known for its cool climate, lush greenery, and breathtaking landscapes. Key attractions include the serene Kodaikanal Lake, the stunning views from Coaker's Walk, and the vibrant blooms of Bryant Park. The town's mist-covered mountains, cascading waterfalls, and tranquil environment make it a perfect retreat for nature lovers and those looking to escape the heat of the plains.


Min-2500|Max-5000

Wayanad


Wayanad, located in the northeastern part of Kerala, is a picturesque district known for its lush green landscapes, dense forests, and rich biodiversity. This enchanting region is characterized by its rolling hills, sprawling plantations, and stunning waterfalls. Major attractions include the Edakkal Caves, which feature ancient rock carvings, the scenic Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, and the serene Pookode Lake. Wayanad is also home to numerous trekking trails, offering breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. Its serene ambiance, coupled with a rich cultural heritage, makes Wayanad a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.


Min-4000|Max-8000

Varkala


Varkala, a picturesque coastal town in Kerala, India, is renowned for its stunning cliffs, pristine beaches, and vibrant cultural scene. Perched along the Arabian Sea, Varkala's unique geography features dramatic red laterite cliffs that offer breathtaking views of the ocean and house numerous resorts, cafes, and shops. Papanasam Beach, also known as Varkala Beach, is famous for its natural springs believed to have medicinal properties and is a popular spot for both relaxation and rejuvenation. The town's spiritual ambiance is heightened by the ancient Janardhana Swamy Temple, a revered pilgrimage site. Varkala seamlessly blends natural beauty, cultural richness, and spiritual serenity, making it a must-visit destination in southern India.
